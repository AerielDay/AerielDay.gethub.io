About Me

Hi, my name is Aeriel Day,
and I am a self-taught developer.
While pursuing a psychology degree in college, I took a beginner's web development course that changed my career path. Web development captivated me with its blend of problem-solving and creative expression.

I quickly decided to shift my focus from psychology to programming. With my brother's advice, I made computer science my minor, allowing me to finish my psychology degree while diving into programming. After college, I learned various programming languages and honed my skills.

To gain practical experience, I became a freelance developer and technician, offering free services in exchange for references. This period was crucial for identifying and improving my weak points, particularly in color theory, with help from my artistically inclined siblings.

Despite my efforts, finding a paid job in the competitive, male-dominated tech industry was challenging. I pursued apprenticeships and landed a temp job using ServiceNow. Through "New Apprenticeship," I received training and secured a full-time role as a ServiceNow platform developer.

I worked in that role for over two years, gaining valuable experience and further training. Now, I am ready for a position beyond an apprenticeship or internship. I strive for continuous improvement and seek a workplace with a culture of growth, a good work-life balance, and a steady flow of new ideas.
